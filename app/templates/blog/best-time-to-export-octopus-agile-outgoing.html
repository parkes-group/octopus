What Is Octopus Agile Outgoing?

Octopus Agile Outgoing is a half-hourly export tariff for households with solar panels. Instead of a fixed rate for every unit you export, you receive a price that changes every 30 minutes.

Export prices follow wholesale electricity markets. When demand is high and supply is tight, you can earn more. When there is surplus renewable generation, prices can fall and sometimes even go negative, meaning you effectively pay to export.

To see live Octopus Agile Outgoing prices for your area, check Agile export prices for your region.

Why Export Prices Change Throughout the Day

Several factors drive octopus export prices time of day:

  • Solar generation — When the sun is strong, many homes export at once. Supply rises and export rates often drop.
  • Electricity demand — Peak demand (late afternoon and evening) pushes wholesale prices up. Exporting then usually earns more.
  • Wholesale market pricing — Agile Outgoing reflects wholesale rates. Spikes and dips flow through to what you earn.

Understanding these patterns helps you time exports to maximise income.

Typical Best Times to Export Solar Power

For many regions, the best time to export solar UK tends to be in the afternoon and evening, when grid demand rises and solar output starts to fall.

Late afternoon (around 4pm–7pm) often sees higher agile outgoing export rates because:

  • People return home and use more electricity
  • Solar generation drops as the sun sets
  • Grid demand peaks, pushing wholesale prices up

Patterns vary by season. Winter evenings can be especially lucrative. Summer midday exports may earn less when solar is abundant. Always check export prices for your region to see current patterns.

Tip: Export prices are often highest in late afternoon and evening when grid demand increases.

How Batteries Can Increase Export Income

A home battery can help you earn more from Agile Outgoing:

  • Charge when import prices are low — Use cheap overnight or midday rates to fill the battery.
  • Export when prices spike — Discharge to the grid during high export windows instead of low ones.
