What Is Fixed Outgoing Octopus?

Fixed Outgoing (also known as Outgoing Octopus) is a simple export tariff. You receive a fixed rate for every unit of electricity you export to the grid—currently around 12p per kWh.

The price is the same regardless of time of day or season. It is simple and predictable.

Fixed Outgoing suits people who want stable income and users without batteries who prefer not to monitor export prices.

What Is Agile Outgoing?

Agile Outgoing is a half-hourly export tariff. Export prices change every 30 minutes and follow the wholesale electricity market.

When demand is high, prices can be higher than fixed tariffs. When supply exceeds demand, prices can fall—and occasionally go negative, meaning you pay to export.

Here is a quick octopus export tariff comparison:

Tariff Typical Export Price Risk Level Best For
Fixed Outgoing ~12p per kWh Low Simple predictable export
Agile Outgoing Variable (0–30p+) Medium Maximising solar income

Fixed outgoing Octopus 12p gives certainty. Agile Outgoing can earn more when you time exports well—but involves more variability.

When Agile Outgoing Pays More

Is Agile Outgoing worth it? In many market conditions, yes. Agile export performs well when:

  • Evening grid demand peaks — Late afternoon and evening often see higher wholesale prices.
  • Winter supply shortages — Colder months can push export rates above fixed levels.
  • High wholesale prices — Spikes flow through to Agile Outgoing.

When Fixed Outgoing May Be Better

Fixed Outgoing can be the better choice when:

  • Export prices are frequently low — If Agile rates often sit below 12p, fixed may earn more.
  • You prefer predictable income — No need to monitor half-hourly prices.
  • You don't want to monitor prices — Set-and-forget suits some households.
